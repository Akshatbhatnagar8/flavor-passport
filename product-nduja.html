Fire from Calabria's Toe

'Nduja (pronounced en-DOO-ya) originates from Spilinga, a small hilltop village in the Vibo Valentia province of Calabria — Italy's southernmost region, scorched by the same sun that ripens its legendary peperoncino. The name derives from the French andouille sausage, brought to the region during Napoleonic rule.

What makes Calabrian 'nduja unique is the peperoncino content — up to 50% by weight. This isn't heat for heat's sake; it's a preservation technique perfected over centuries in the sweltering south, where the chilli effectively cures the meat. Ours is aged for a minimum of 90 days in natural casings in a stone cellar in the Aspromonte foothills.
