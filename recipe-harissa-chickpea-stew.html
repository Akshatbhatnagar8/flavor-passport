Harissa Chermoula
Chickpea Stew with Couscous

A one-pot North African classic that's entirely plant-based and fully ready in 30 minutes. Courgette, peppers and chickpeas simmered in a smoky tomato base fired up with Harissa Chermoula, then served over herb-flecked couscous with pomegranate and dried apricot.

Prep10 min
Cook20 min
Total30 min
Serves2
DifficultyEasy

Harissa Chermoula

The original recipe calls for both harissa paste and a separate ras-el-hanout spice sachet. Our Harissa Chermoula already combines the fire of harissa with the herb complexity of chermoula — two steps in one spoonful. No extra spice blend needed.

How to make it

1

Prep the vegetables

Trim the courgette and cut into 2cm half-moons. Halve the peppers, remove seeds, and cut into 2cm chunks. Drain and rinse the chickpeas thoroughly.

2

Fry the vegetables

Heat a generous glug of olive oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the courgette and pepper and fry for 3–4 minutes, stirring regularly, until the edges begin to soften and colour slightly.

💡 Don't stir too much — you want some caramelisation on the courgette for depth of flavour.
3

Add the harissa & simmer

Add the chickpeas, chopped tomatoes, 2 tbsp Flavor Passport Harissa Chermoula, and a splash of water. Season with salt. Stir well. Bring to the boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 14–16 minutes until the sauce thickens and the vegetables are fully tender. Taste and add more Harissa Chermoula if you want more heat.

💡 Because our paste already includes chermoula herbs, you won't need ras-el-hanout or extra spices.
4

Cook the couscous

While the stew simmers, combine the couscous and vegetable stock paste in a bowl. Pour over 160ml boiling water, stir, then cover with a plate or cling film. Leave for 8–10 minutes undisturbed, then fluff with a fork. Stir in half the chopped parsley and season.

5

Plate & garnish

Spoon the herbed couscous into wide bowls. Ladle the stew generously on top. Scatter the dried apricot pieces, pomegranate seeds, and remaining parsley. Serve immediately — the pomegranate juice will run into the stew as you eat.

Ingredients — serves 2

🌶️ 2 tbsp Harissa Chermoula
Stew
  • 1 courgette
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 tin chickpeas (400g), drained
  • 1 tin finely chopped tomatoes (400g)
  • 1 tbsp water
  • Olive oil, salt & pepper
Couscous
  • 160g couscous
  • 1 tsp vegetable stock paste
  • 160ml boiling water
  • Small bunch flat-leaf parsley
To serve
  • 40g dried apricots, roughly chopped
  • Seeds of ½ pomegranate

Make it your own

  • Add a dollop of plain yoghurt on top to cool the heat
  • Swap couscous for bulgur wheat or quinoa
  • Stir in a handful of spinach at the end for greens
  • Keeps well in the fridge for 3 days — even better the next day
