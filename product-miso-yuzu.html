Miso Yuzu Glaze

Umami-rich, with a bright citrus finish

Fermented white miso meets hand-pressed yuzu citrus from family orchards in Shikoku. Cold-mixed, never heated — preserving every enzyme and live culture.

Ingredients

  • White miso paste
  • Hand-pressed yuzu juice
  • Mirin
  • Rice vinegar
  • Ginger
  • Sesame oil
  • Sea salt

Flavor Profile

Umami Citrus Sweet Mellow Savory

How to Use

  1. 1
    🐟 Glaze for salmon or chicken — brush on during the last 3 minutes of cooking for a caramelised, glossy finish.
  2. 2
    🥗 Salad dressing base — thin with rice vinegar and a splash of sesame oil for an instant Japanese-style vinaigrette.
  3. 3
    🍜 Ramen topping or stir-fry finisher — a spoonful stirred in at the very end adds depth without overpowering the dish.
  4. 4
    🥢 Dipping sauce — serve alongside gyoza, spring rolls, or steamed dumplings with a little chili oil on the side.
Origin Story

Fermented in Kyoto.
Picked in Shikoku.

The Nakamura family has been fermenting miso in Kyoto since 1954. Their yuzu grove in Shikoku adds citrus notes no industrial process can replicate. Harvested October–November, the yuzu is cold-pressed within 24 hours of picking — this is why the glaze carries that unmistakable fresh citrus lift that you simply cannot get from concentrate.

Coastal climate of Shikoku gives yuzu its distinctive floral-citrus character, grown at sea level where ocean humidity influences the rind's essential oils.
🏺
White shiro miso fermented for 30–60 days in cedar barrels — a shorter ferment than red miso, producing the sweeter, more delicate base profile.
❄️
Cold-blended, never heated — the final mixing process uses no heat, preserving live enzymes and the volatile citrus aromatics from the fresh-pressed yuzu.
