Salsa Macha

Dried chillies, toasted nuts, sesame & dark chocolate notes

Hailing from the Gulf Coast state of Veracruz, Salsa Macha is Mexico's answer to chilli oil. Slow-fried dried chillies in olive oil with peanuts, pumpkin seeds and a whisper of dark chocolate. Rich, nutty and dangerously addictive.

From the Gulf Coast of Veracruz

Salsa Macha has roots in the indigenous Totonac culture of Veracruz, where dried chillies were preserved in oil as a method of extending shelf life through the rainy season. The addition of nuts and seeds came later, influenced by the region's rich trade history with the Spanish colonial spice routes.

Unlike most Mexican salsas, Salsa Macha is cooked — chillies and garlic are slowly fried in oil until caramelised and deeply smoky, then blended with the toasted nuts and seeds. Ours includes a touch of cacao nibs, a nod to Veracruz's pre-Hispanic chocolate tradition.
