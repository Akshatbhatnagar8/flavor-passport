Peruvian Deviled Eggs
with Aji Amarillo

The humble deviled egg gets a passport upgrade. Creamy yolk filling spiked with our Aji Amarillo Paste delivers a fruity, medium heat that turns a party staple into a conversation starter. Bright golden colour, rich flavour, impossibly easy.

Prep20 min
Cook10 min
Total30 min
Serves12 people
DifficultyEasy

The Flavor Passport shortcut

🌕

Aji Amarillo Paste

The original recipe uses aji amarillo paste as its star ingredient. Ours is slow-roasted in the Sacred Valley at 2,800m altitude — fruiter and more complex than most commercial versions. It gives the filling that iconic golden-orange glow and a warm, building heat.

How to make it

1

Hard-boil the eggs

Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Using a slotted spoon, lower the eggs in gently to avoid cracking. Boil for exactly 10 minutes. Drain and run under cold water until completely cool — this stops the yolks from turning grey.

💡 Shake the drained pot gently to crack all the shells at once — it makes peeling much faster.
2

Peel the eggs

Peel each egg starting from the wide bottom end — there's an air pocket there that makes it easier to get under the shell. Rinse under cold water to remove any shell fragments, then dry with kitchen paper.

3

Slice & scoop

Cut each egg lengthways with a sharp knife. Using a teaspoon, scoop the yolks cleanly into a bowl. Arrange the empty whites on a serving platter or over a bed of lettuce.

4

Make the filling

Mash the yolks thoroughly with a fork until no lumps remain. Add the mayonnaise and 1½ tbsp Flavor Passport Aji Amarillo Paste. Stir until completely smooth and the colour is an even golden-orange. Season with salt and pepper. Taste — the paste has a building heat, so add more gradually if you want more fire.

💡 For an extra-silky filling, pass the mixture through a fine sieve before piping.
5

Fill & garnish

Spoon the filling into the egg whites, or use a piping bag with a star tip for a polished look. Dust lightly with smoked paprika. Serve immediately, or refrigerate for up to 2 hours before serving.

Ingredients — 24 halves

🌕 1½ tbsp Aji Amarillo Paste
  • 12 large eggs
  • ¾ cup (170g) good-quality mayonnaise
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Smoked paprika, to garnish

Serving ideas

  • Top with a small slice of avocado and a micro-herb
  • Add a few drops of lime juice to the filling for brightness
  • Garnish with crispy fried shallots for texture
  • Make ahead: fill up to 4 hours before, refrigerate covered
