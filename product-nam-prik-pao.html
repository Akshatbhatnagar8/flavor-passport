Nam Prik Pao

Roasted chilli jam with shrimp paste & palm sugar

Thailand's iconic roasted chilli paste — sweet, smoky and deeply savoury all at once. A staple of Thai pantries for centuries, Nam Prik Pao transforms simple dishes with layers of caramelised complexity.

How to Use

  • 1Stir into Tom Yum or Tom Kha soup — just one tablespoon transforms the broth.
  • 2Toss with pad see ew noodles for a smoky, caramelised base.
  • 3Spread on toast with a fried egg for a quick Thai-inspired breakfast.
  • 4Use as a dipping sauce for grilled prawns or crispy pork belly.

From Bangkok's Street Markets

Nam Prik Pao — literally "roasted chilli paste" — has been a cornerstone of Thai cooking since the Ayutthaya period (14th–18th century). Originally prepared by fire-roasting chillis, shallots and garlic directly on charcoal, its smoky character is inseparable from its identity.

Our version is made in a small family-run factory in Nonthaburi, using traditional clay pots and palm sugar harvested from the coconut palms of Samut Songkhram. No shortcuts, no artificial smoke — just slow, careful roasting.
