Sheet Pan Miso Yuzu
Glazed Salmon & Bok Choy

One pan, 30 minutes, zero compromise. Wild salmon fillets brushed generously with our Miso Yuzu Glaze, roasted alongside sesame-dressed bok choy. The glaze caramelises under the broiler into a lacquered, umami-rich crust.

Prep15 min
Cook15 min
Total30 min
Serves4
DifficultyEasy

The Flavor Passport shortcut

🍯

Miso Yuzu Glaze

The original recipe requires 6 separate ingredients for the glaze. Our Miso Yuzu Glaze does it all — fermented white miso, hand-pressed yuzu, honey and sesame already balanced. Just open and brush.

How to make it

1

Preheat & prep

Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Line half a sheet pan with parchment paper. Pat the salmon fillets dry with kitchen paper and season the tops with salt and cracked black pepper.

2

Glaze the salmon

Brush the top and sides of each salmon fillet generously with Flavor Passport Miso Yuzu Glaze. Reserve about 1 tablespoon for a final brush at the end. Let the salmon rest for 15 minutes to absorb the glaze — this makes a real difference to the depth of flavour.

💡 The longer you marinate, the deeper the caramelisation. Up to 2 hours in the fridge is even better.
3

Dress the bok choy

Halve the Shanghai bok choy lengthways. Whisk together the garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and sugar. Brush this mixture over the bok choy faces, working it between the leaves. Drizzle the empty half of the sheet pan with avocado oil and lay the bok choy face-side up.

4

Roast together

Place the glazed salmon on the parchment half. Put the whole pan in the oven. After 6 minutes, flip the bok choy. Continue roasting for another 6–8 minutes until the bok choy stems are tender and the salmon reaches an internal temperature of 52°C (125°F).

5

Broil to finish

Brush the reserved Miso Yuzu Glaze over the salmon. Switch the oven to grill/broil and cook for 1–2 minutes until the glaze is slightly charred and glistening. Watch closely — it goes from perfect to burnt fast.

💡 That char is the goal. The sugars in the glaze caramelise and create an addictive bittersweet crust.
6

Plate & serve

Serve over steamed jasmine or sushi rice. Scatter toasted sesame seeds and sliced spring onions over the salmon. Add black sesame seeds over the bok choy. Eat immediately.

Ingredients — serves 4

🍯 3 tbsp Flavor Passport Miso Yuzu Glaze
Salmon
  • 4 salmon fillets (approx. 170g each), skin-on
  • Salt & cracked black pepper
  • Toasted sesame seeds, to garnish
  • 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
Bok Choy
  • 4 Shanghai bok choy, halved lengthways
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • ½ tbsp avocado or neutral oil
  • Black sesame seeds, to garnish
To serve
  • Steamed jasmine rice

Chef's tips

  • Use wild salmon for best flavour — farmed works but the fat content means longer cook times
  • If the glaze starts burning before the fish is cooked, tent loosely with foil
  • Leftovers flake beautifully into a rice bowl the next day
