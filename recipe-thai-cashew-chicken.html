Thai Cashew Chicken
with Nam Prik Pao

The Thai restaurant classic you can nail at home in under 30 minutes. Juicy chicken thighs, tricolour peppers, and crunchy cashews tossed in a glossy sauce built on Nam Prik Pao — the smoky, caramelised chilli jam at the heart of Thai cooking.

Prep15 min
Cook15 min
Total30 min
Serves2
DifficultyEasy

The Flavor Passport shortcut

🌺

Nam Prik Pao

The original calls for nam prik pao as the sauce backbone, plus dried chilies for extra heat. Our Nam Prik Pao is made in Nonthaburi using coconut husk charcoal-roasted chilies and stone-ground palm sugar — it carries both the heat and the smoke, so you can skip the dried chilies entirely.

How to make it

1

Marinate the chicken

Cut the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces. Toss with 2 tsp soy sauce and set aside while you prep everything else. This quick marinade seasons the chicken all the way through.

2

Toast the cashews

Spread the cashews on a baking tray and roast at 180°C (350°F) for 8–12 minutes until golden and fragrant. Watch them closely — they can go from golden to burnt in under a minute. Set aside.

💡 You can also toast them in a dry wok over medium heat for 4–5 minutes, stirring constantly.
3

Mix the sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together: 2 tbsp Flavor Passport Nam Prik Pao, oyster sauce, soy sauce, fish sauce, palm sugar, and sesame oil. Taste and adjust — the nam prik pao already carries the chilli heat, so no dried chilies needed.

4

Sear the chicken

Heat oil in a wok or wide frying pan over high heat until smoking. Add the chicken pieces and sear for 1–2 minutes per side until nicely browned but not cooked through. Remove from the pan and set aside. The carryover cooking will finish them later.

💡 High heat is essential here. If the pan isn't properly hot, the chicken will steam instead of sear.
5

Stir-fry the vegetables

In the same wok, stir-fry the onion for 1 minute. Add the garlic and the white parts of the spring onions and cook for 30 seconds. Add the peppers and stir-fry for 2 minutes, adding a small splash of water and covering briefly to steam until just tender.

6

Finish & serve

Return the chicken to the wok. Pour over the Nam Prik Pao sauce and toss everything to coat. Add the cashews and spring onion greens. Turn off the heat immediately — the residual heat will finish the chicken perfectly. Serve over jasmine rice.

Ingredients — serves 2

🌺 2 tbsp Nam Prik Pao
Main
  • 300g boneless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 60g unsalted cashews
  • 1 green, 1 red, 1 yellow bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 spring onions, white and green separated
  • 2 tbsp neutral cooking oil
Sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp fish sauce
  • ½ tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 2 tsp soy sauce (for marinating)
To serve
  • Steamed jasmine rice

Tips & swaps

  • Swap chicken for prawns — reduce searing time to 1 min per side
  • Use peanuts instead of cashews for a more traditional version
  • Add a fried egg on top of the rice for extra richness
  • Vegetarian? Use firm tofu and vegetarian oyster sauce
