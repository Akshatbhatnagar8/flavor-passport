Origin Story

Grown at 3,400m.
Cusco's Sacred Valley.

The González family has grown aji amarillo at 3,400m altitude in the Sacred Valley for five generations. The elevation gives the pepper its distinctive fruity heat — at high altitude, the pepper's skin thins, concentrating the capsaicin and allowing the tropical fruit notes to develop fully. Cold-pressed within hours of harvest to capture the volatile aromatic compounds that make this paste so unlike any other chili product.
